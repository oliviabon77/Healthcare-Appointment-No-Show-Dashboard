# Healthcare Appointment No-Show Dashboard

## Overview
Analysis of 110,527 patient appointments to exploring patterns associated with no-show behavior across a healthcare clinic network. The dashboard was designed to help visualize operational patterns related to appointment attendance, scheduling behavior, and patient communication in order to better understand .

## Business Questions Answered
- What is the overall no show rate across appointments?
- How does scheduling lead time affect no show probability?
- Do SMS reminders reduce no show rates?
- Which days of the week were associated with the highest no-show rates

## Key Findings
- Overall no-show rate was 20.2% across 110,527 appointments
- Longer scheduling delays were associated with higher no-show probability
- Patients who received SMS reminders showed a 27.6% no-show rate versus 
  16.7% for those who did not, suggesting reminders may be targeted toward 
  higher-risk patients rather than reducing risk directly
- Saturday appointments had the highest no-show rates

## Operational Implications & Future Improvements
-The results suggest that missed appointments are connected to several factors rather than one single cause.
-Scheduling delays, reminder outreach, and appointment timing all appeared to relate to changes in attendance patterns. These trends could help clinics better recognize appointments that may carry a higher risk of no-shows and improve scheduling decisions over time.
-Future work on this project will include adding more dashboard pages, looking at how multiple variables relate to attendance behavior, and continuing to explore differences across patient groups and appointment types.
